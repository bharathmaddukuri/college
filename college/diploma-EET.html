Diploma in Electrical Engineering Technology

PROGRAM EDUCATIONAL OBJECTIVES

Electrical and electronic engineering lies in the heart of modern society; they are closely related areas that serve an important role in our everyday lives and in the function of engineering businesses. The Electrical Engineering Technology program at the American College of the Middle East (ACM) covers a wide range of general engineering principles and concepts.

One of the main strengths of the program is the practical aspect and use of the computer software tools that allow you to develop hands-on competence and problem analysis. The program also aims to develop your interpersonal, teamwork and IT skills. Moreover, our internship program provides excellent exposure to the practical experience and the skills required for the future career.

The program also prepares you to join an undergraduate education program leading to a Bachelor degree in Electrical and Electronic Engineering. In addition, graduates have the choice to pursue advanced technical certificates and diplomas.

STUDENT LEARNING GOALS
  • A commitment to quality, timeliness, and continuous improvement.
  • Ability to identify and conduct experiments, as well as to analyze and interpret data.
  • Ability to identify, analyzes, and solves defined engineering technology problems.
  • Ability to apply written, oral, and graphical communication in both technical and non-technical environments.
  • Ability to engage to engage in self-directed continuing professional development.
  • Ability to apply the knowledge of mathematics, science, and engineering-to-engineering technology problems.
  • Ability to apply the knowledge, techniques, skills, and modern tools of the discipline to narrowly define engineering technology activities.
  • Ability to communicate effectively.
  • A commitment to understanding and being able to address professional and ethical responsibilities, including a respect for diversity.
CAREER OPPORTUNITIES

Electrical engineering jobs are not restricted to engineering industries. Instead graduates may pursue careers in many other industries, which include: automotive, chemical, electronics, oil and gas, power generation and telecommunications industries.
