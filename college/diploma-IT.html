Diploma in Information Technology

PROGRAM EDUCATIONAL OBJECTIVES

The College’s program in Information Technology focuses on implementing computer technology to fit the needs and demands of businesses and organizations. Students will learn how to use computer networks to solve problems and increase communication. The program provides students with the technical skills necessary to install, configure, manage, support, and implement network infrastructure for small, medium, and large enterprise environment. The focus is on hands-on learning techniques as technology is handled and actual computer networks are built.

As students graduate, and professional development continue, the College also prepares them to pursue an undergraduate education program which leads to a Bachelor’s in Information Technology. In addition, the program, will provide graduates with sound preparation for various certification exams, such as: Microsoft Certified System Administrator (MCSA), Computer Technology Industry Association’s A+, Network+, Security+, or equivalent as different industry certification evolves.

STUDENT LEARNING GOALS
  • Communicate effectively with customers, supervisors and peers, both orally and in writing
  • Demonstrate independent problem-solving skills
  • Manage projects, change, resources, and conflict
  • Use and apply information systems solutions
  • Understand and apply high-level tools and emerging technologies
  • Use and apply systems theory and concepts
  • Understand and apply system development methodologies
  • Understand the expectations of an Information Technology professional
  • Work effectively in teams to achieve a common goal
CAREER OPPORTUNITIES

IT departments are found in nearly all medium and large organizations. Graduates may pursue careers in Network Administration, Help Desk & Technical Support, Systems Analysis, and Network Security.
