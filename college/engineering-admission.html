Engineering

The admission process at the ACM is designed to meet its educational philosophy that targets serious and dedicated students, who possess the talents of achievement and execution. The college is committed to multiple methods of assessment and evaluation that would allow a large segment of students to apply and yet demonstrate needed skills that guarantee their success and suitability to the program.

Application Requirements for the American College of the Middle East – Engineering Program

  • Must be a highschool graduate certified by the Kuwait Ministry of Education Science.
  • No minimum grade point average or class rank is required to apply or to be admitted to the ACM.
  • A score of level (1) or above on the English Language Assessment Test (specific to ACM)
  • A score of 40% or above on the Math Assessment Test (specific to ACM)

Note: failure to meet one of the requirements above will lead to the rejection of the application.

Note: Admission tests will not be repeated, except for the English Language Assessment Test, if applicants have "level B". In this case, applicants may repeat the test only once.

Admission requirements

After the application process ACM evaluates applicants according to a weighted-score admission system. Applicants receive their weighted-score based on their performance in the admission criteria below. The total weighted score called the Admission Assessment Grade (AAG) determines whether the applicant is admitted or not. A minimum of 55% or above must be achieved in the AAG, if applicant’s score is below 55% the applicant will not be admitted. The AAG consists of the following:

A. High School GPA:
GPA weight is 40% of the total AAG.

B. English Language Skills Assessment:
English Language skills test weight is 20% of the total AAG.

C. Math Skills Assessment:
Math skills test weight is 20% of the total AAG.

D. Communication Skills Assessment:
Communication skills test weight is 20% of the total AAG.

Placement & Registration

Admitted students will acquire confirmed admission status and will be granted direct entry to college level course if they achieve the following:

1. Achieve one of the following English test scores:

  • Level 3 or above in the English Language Assessment test
  • 550 or above on the International TOEFL
  • 5.5 or above in the Academics IELTS

The deadline for submission of official International TOEFL or Academic IELTS is the last day of the add/drop period of the semester.

2. Achieve minimum 70% or above on the Math Assessment Test

The deadline for submission of official International TOEFL or Academic IELTS is the last day of the add/drop period of the semester.
