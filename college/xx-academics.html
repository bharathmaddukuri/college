Academics

  • 1. DIPLOMA IN BUSINESS ADMINISTRATION – ACCOUNTINGOpen or Close
    PROGRAM EDUCATIONAL OBJECTIVES

    The Accounting Program at the American College of the Middle East (ACM) is committed to provide students with the general educational and specific technical knowledge necessary to enter the accounting profession and pursue a successful professional career.

    We at ACM believe that in an increasingly complex business environment, a successful accountant must possess broad knowledge of the business field to be able to utilize sound and professional judgment. An accountant must have essential business skills to communicate verbally and in writing, as well as the ability to work in teams, motivate others, and manage people and tasks in an organized and well-planned method. Consequently, the program aims to arm its students with knowledge of basic accounting concepts, accounting applications, and recognized accounting business practices.

    As students graduate, and professional development continues, the College also prepares them to pursue an undergraduate education program which leads to a Bachelor’s in Accounting, in addition to acquiring technical advanced certificates and diplomas.

    STUDENT LEARNING GOALS
    • Achieve understanding and application of essential content in accounting
      • To attain a level of understanding and application related to financial and managerial accounting
      • Develop competency to develop, measure, analyze, validate and communicate financial and other information
    • Develop an awareness and knowledge of the role of ethics in the accounting profession and in accounting business practices
    • Develop effective written and oral communication skills
    • Develop the leadership and teamwork skills to function effectively in a diverse cross-functional environment
    • Familiarize with local business methods and accounting standards
    • To acquire professional experience working in an accounting role
    • Understand current business technologies and software in essential tasks
    CAREER OPPORTUNITIES

    Students pursuing an accounting concentration at ACM are offered challenging and rewarding opportunities in accounting and auditing, in both, government and private commercial enterprises. Graduates may pursue careers in Administrative Management, Office Management, Administrative Support, and Customer Service.

  • 2. DIPLOMA IN BUSINESS ADMINISTRATION – FINANCIAL SERVICESOpen or Close
    PROGRAM EDUCATIONAL OBJECTIVES

    The objective of the Financial Services Program at the American College of the Middle East (ACM) is to develop the foundation for individuals to become professional financial services practitioners. They will be able to build practices in financial planning, investments & securities, and treasury management.

    The program is designed to provide an overview of the various financial services organizations, their service offerings, and the essential role they play within the economy. Our curriculum is built on the solid synergy of various business theories and financial techniques. Students will gain applied knowledge and on-the-job skills through internships, projects, hands-on activities, and related industry assignments.

    As students graduate, and professional development continue, the College also prepares them to pursue an undergraduate education program which leads to a Bachelor’s in Finance, in addition to acquiring technical advanced certificates and diplomas.

    STUDENT LEARNING GOALS
    • To achieve depth of understanding and application of essential content in finance
    • Acquire professional experience working in a finance role
    • Develop effective written and oral communication skills
    • Develop the leadership and teamwork skills to function effectively in a diverse cross-functional environment
    • Develop an awareness and knowledge of the role of ethics in the financial profession and in finance and investment business practices
    • Students will be prepared to analyze a situation in the financial services and assess possible solutions
    • Students will understand the role of financial policy and investment analysis
    • Students will learn the dynamics of stock markets, asset valuation, portfolio construction and risk management
    • Use analytical and quantitative skills to analyze and formulate business decisions
    CAREER OPPORTUNITIES

    Graduates will be well suited to work in financial services jobs in a variety of financial organizations including banks, investment companies, and insurance companies. They will also be able to apply their skills to develop a successful independent financial services practice.

  • 3. DIPLOMA IN BUSINESS ADMINISTRATION – HUMAN RESOURCES MANAGEMENTOpen or Close
    PROGRAM EDUCATIONAL OBJECTIVES

    The Human Resources Management Program at the American College of the Middle East (ACM) primarily equips the graduates with broad knowledge and solid execution skills to start and develop a career in this field. The HRM function has undergone tremendous change over the past years; today's human resources professionals are at the heart of modern business, ensuring the fair treatment of individuals in an organization and fulfilling an important role in a company’s performance.

    The program will prepare ACM’s graduates to implement different HR tasks such as: maintaining personnel records; administering aptitude tests & training programs; assisting in the hiring process; answering employee inquiries; preparing reports for managers; and screening applicants. Graduates will have the ability to apply all of the above by utilizing the best IT functional technologies through internships, projects, hands-on activities, and related industry assignments.

    As students graduate, and professional development continue, the College also prepares them to pursue an undergraduate education program which leads to a Bachelor’s in Human Resource Management, in addition to acquiring technical advanced certificates and diplomas.

    3.1.2 STUDENT LEARNING GOALS
    • Achieve understanding and application of essential content in human resource management
    • Develop an awareness and knowledge of the role of ethics in the HR profession and in HR business practices
    • Develop effective written and oral communication skills
    • Familiarize with local and global business methods
    • To acquire understanding of human resource management and its role in the organization
    • To have an understanding for Kuwait employment and labor laws
    • To achieve understanding of hiring and selection techniques, performance management systems, compensation and benefits systems and training and development processes
    • To acquire professional experience working in an HR role
    3.1.3 CAREER OPPORTUNITIES

    HR departments are found in all sectors, and in nearly all medium and large organizations. Graduates may pursue careers in HR Coordination, Personnel, Training, Employee Relations, and Recruitment.

  • 4. DIPLOMA IN BUSINESS ADMINISTRATION – MARKETINGOpen or Close

    4.1 DEGREE DESCRIPTION:

    4.1.1 PROGRAM EDUCATIONAL OBJECTIVES

    The study of Marketing is an exciting field that introduces students to all aspects of marketing, from product conception to product consumption, thus opening students up to countless opportunities in the industry. Tailored to blend creative applied knowledge with a grounding of business, marketing and contextual studies, it provides students with career preparation for entry and mid-level positions in marketing.

    The program gives students appropriate knowledge and skills to understand the function of marketing in the firm and in society by introducing students to the fundamentals of marketing and how to implement marketing plans. Courses are designed to develop students’ abilities to analyze, plan, implement, and control the creation of demand among targeted buyers. This program of study encompasses a wide range of marketing activities, such as advertising, packaging, promotion, and distribution.

    As students graduate, and professional development continue, the College also prepares them to pursue an undergraduate education program which leads to a Bachelor’s in Marketing, in addition to acquiring technical advanced certificates and diplomas.

    4.1.2 STUDENT LEARNING GOALS
    • To achieve depth of understanding and application of essential content in marketing
    • Develop competency to develop, measure, analyze, validate and communicate business information
    • Develop effective written and oral communication skills.
    • Develop the leadership and teamwork skills to function effectively in a diverse cross-functional environment.
    • Develop an awareness and knowledge of the role of ethics in the marketing profession and in marketing business practices
    • Familiarize with local and global business methods
    • To develop an understanding of what marketing/marketing management is and what decisions are involved in regards to the marketing process, market segmentation/positioning and marketing mix
    • To understand the responsibilities and tasks of a marketing manager
    • To understand marketing planning processes, and customer relationship management.
    • To acquire professional experience working in a marketing role
    4.1.3 CAREER OPPORTUNITIES

    Marketing departments are found in nearly all medium and large organizations. Graduates may pursue careers in Marketing Coordination, Advertising, Promotion, Public Relations, and Product Development.

  • 5. DIPLOMA IN COMPUTER NETWORK SYSTEMS - CNSOpen or Close

    5.1 DEGREE DESCRIPTION:

    5.1.1 PROGRAM EDUCATIONAL OBJECTIVES

    The college’s programs in computer network systems focuses on implementing computer networking technology to fit the needs and demands of businesses and organizations. Students will learn how to use computer networks to solve problems and increase communication. The program provides students with the technical skills necessary to install, configure, manage, support, and implement network infrastructure for small, medium, and large enterprise environment. The focus is on hands-on learning techniques as technology is handled and actual computer networks are built.

    As students graduate, and professional development continue, the College also prepares them to pursue an undergraduate education program which leads to a Bachelor’s in Information Technology, in addition to acquiring technical advanced certificates and diplomas, such as: Microsoft Certified System Administrator (MCSA), Computer Technology Industry Association’s A+, Network+, Security+, or equivalent as different industry certification evolves.

    5.1.2 STUDENT LEARNING GOALS
    • Communicate effectively with customers, supervisors and peers, both orally and in writing
    • Demonstrate independent problem-solving skills
    • Manage projects, change, resources, and conflict
    • Use and apply information systems solutions
    • Use and apply systems theory and concepts
    • Understand and apply high-level tools and emerging technologies
    • Understand the expectations of an Information Technology professional
    • Understand and apply system development methodologies
    • Work effectively in teams to achieve a common goal
    5.1.3 CAREER OPPORTUNITIES

    IT departments are found in nearly all medium and large organizations. Graduates may pursue careers in Network Administration, Help Desk & Technical Support, Systems Analysis, and Network Security.

  • 7. DIPLOMA IN ELECTRICAL ENGINEERING TECHNOLOGY -EETOpen or Close

    7.1 DEGREE DESCRIPTION:

    7.1.1 PROGRAM EDUCATIONAL OBJECTIVES

    Electrical and electronic engineering lies in the heart of modern society; they are closely related areas that serve an important role in our everyday lives and in the function of engineering businesses. The Electrical Engineering Technology program at the American College of the Middle East (ACM) covers a wide range of general engineering principles and concepts.

    One of the main strengths of the program is the practical aspect and use of the computer software tools that allow you to develop hands-on competence and problem analysis. The program also aims to develop your interpersonal, teamwork and IT skills. Moreover, our internship program provides excellent exposure to the practical experience and the skills required for the future career.

    The program also prepares you to join an undergraduate education program leading to a Bachelor degree in Electrical and Electronic Engineering. In addition, graduates have the choice to pursue advanced technical certificates and diplomas.

    7.1.2 STUDENT LEARNING GOALS
    • A commitment to quality, timeliness, and continuous improvement.
    • Ability to identify and conduct experiments, as well as to analyze and interpret data.
    • Ability to identify, analyzes, and solves defined engineering technology problems.
    • Ability to apply written, oral, and graphical communication in both technical and non-technical environments.
    • Ability to engage to engage in self-directed continuing professional development.
    • Ability to apply the knowledge of mathematics, science, and engineering-to-engineering technology problems.
    • Ability to apply the knowledge, techniques, skills, and modern tools of the discipline to narrowly define engineering technology activities.
    • Ability to communicate effectively.
    • A commitment to understanding and being able to address professional and ethical responsibilities, including a respect for diversity.
    7.1.3 CAREER OPPORTUNITIES

    Electrical engineering jobs are not restricted to engineering industries. Instead graduates may pursue careers in many other industries, which include: automotive, chemical, electronics, oil and gas, power generation and telecommunications industries.
